Product Description

Ginger is a common spice known for its pungent, slightly sweet flavor and medicinal properties. It is typically used fresh, dried, pickled, or powdered in a variety of culinary and therapeutic applications.

Key Features

  • Freshness: Typically, fresh ginger should have a firm texture and a strong, aromatic smell. Ensure the product is fresh when buying.
  • Packaging: Usually sold in a raw, unprocessed form; may come in plastic or paper packaging.

Uses

  • Culinary Uses: Adds flavor to dishes such as soups, stews, curries, and stir-fries. It can also be used in beverages like ginger tea or ginger ale.
  • Medicinal Uses: Commonly used in traditional medicine for its potential benefits in digestion, reducing nausea, and anti-inflammatory properties.
  • Other Uses: Can be used in baking, as a spice mix ingredient, or as a natural remedy for colds and sore throats.

Benefits

  • Digestive Health: Aids in digestion and helps alleviate nausea.
  • Anti-inflammatory: Contains compounds like gingerol which have anti-inflammatory effects.
  • Immune Support: May boost the immune system and help fight off infections.
  • Pain Relief: Can help reduce muscle pain and soreness.

Storage

  • Fresh Ginger: Store in a cool, dry place. Can also be kept in the refrigerator to extend shelf life. It should be used within a few weeks.
  • Ground Ginger: Keep in an airtight container in a cool, dry place to maintain its potency.

Nutritional Information

  • Calories: 80 kcal per 100 grams
  • Carbohydrates: 18 grams
  • Fiber: 2 grams
  • Protein: 2 grams
  • Fat: 0.7 grams
  • Vitamins & Minerals: Contains small amounts of vitamins like Vitamin B6, Vitamin C, and minerals like potassium and magnesium.
